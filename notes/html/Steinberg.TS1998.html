Steinberg, Marc. 1998. "Tilting the Frame: Considerations on Collective Action Framing from a Discursive Turn." TS 27(6).

  1. Introduction
    • Given the focus on meaning in frame analysis, surprising that there is not mention of discourse. This highlights some key problems within the internal logic of frame analysis and more generally its capacity to analyze the contentious process of meaning production.
    • Criticisms -- what is the relation of ideology and frames, i.e. where do they come from in terms of other culture; tendency to reify frames; the relation of micromobilization and macro structures remains ambiguous; frames remain internally coherent and stable, but this is not the case; and frames cannot be thought of as a resource, highlighting tension between rational choice and constructionist views of discourse.
    • Need to think of instead of "discursive fields" -- like collective action repertoires, discursive repetoires develop interactionally with opponents and targets.
