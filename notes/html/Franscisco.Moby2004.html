Franciso, Ronald A. "After the massacre: Mobilization in the wake of harsh repression." Mobilization 9(2).

  1. Introduction
    • Not much research about what happens after a massacre.
  2. Repression events
    • Including events that have (1) non-violent unarmed dissidents in (2) a non-democratic country who are (3) shot, killed, or injured in a discrete incident by state forces with at least three are killed and a total set of causalities over 30. Genocide is excluded.
    • Large amount of backlash taking place after most massacres.
  3. Collective-action theory and post-massacre backlash
    • Information transmission about a massacre
      • needing sufficient amount of this
    • Leadership
      • Generating adaptive tactics to overcome resistance to protest.
    • Risks of backlash
      • Biggest risk is identities of protesters becoming known.
  4. Mobilization
    • But why would any individual act against the state after a massacre
    • Using Bayesian updating to test. If there are deaths and injuries on post-massacre days 1 and 2, probably less likely to be mobilization on day three.
