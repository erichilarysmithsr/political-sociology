Swidler, Ann. 1986. "Culture in Action: Symbols and Strategies." ASR 51.

Notes from Ermakoff's seminar, Fall 2010

  1. Key claim
    • In unsettled times, explicit ideologies directly govern action.
      • Buy why?
    • Ideology defined here as a symbolic system, derived from Geertz.
      • Geertz was the first to propose the concept of ideology as a symbolic system, regardless of its political content.
      • From the moment one is self-conscious of their worldview, s/he has an ideology
    • In times of crisis, people lose their bearings. Meaning becomes problematic. Ideology, then, provides one with a roadmap; they self-consciously endorse meaning systems because they shape action in a world that lacks meaning.
  2. Criticisms
    1. The definition and distinction between settled and unsettled times.
      • Intuitively, the concept of unsettled times is a conjuncture in which institutions have broken down.
      • In settled times, culture is integrated with action; in unsettled times there is a disjuncture between culture and action. Ideology shapes action.
      • However, Swidler makes a tautological definition of unsettled times - they are the times in which ideology shapes action - exactly what she is trying to show.
    2. The functional argument that ideology provides meaning in times in which meaning is lack has no theory of motivation.
      • What drives people to adopt motivation?
      • It could be possible that actions occur, then people adopt an ideology post hoc to rationalize their actions that align with pre-existing beliefs.
      • Swidler makes this an argument of motivation, however.
