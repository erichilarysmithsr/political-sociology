Rothman, Franklin Daniel and Pamela Oliver. 1999. "From local to global: the anti-dam movement in Southern Brazil, 1979-1992." Mobilization 4(1).

  1. Introduction
    • Highlighting anti-dam movement in Brazil.
  2. Theoretical framework
    • Nested opportunity structures - multiple levels of political and economic structures which provide local character and constraints to local manifestations of social movements.
    • Clarifying relations between internal and external - International elites often fund local movements in order to undercut or circumvent national governments.
      • Important to distinguish between different international actors.
    • Specifying how frames and identities change with opportunity structures - frame alignment entitled a wholesale reworking of their values and beliefs.
  3. Methods
    • 100 interviews in Brazil. Analysis of movement files.
  4. Dam resistance as a land struggle: 1979-1987
  5. The construction of new alliances and ideology: 1988-1992
    • Effectively the meeting up with democratic transition happening worldwide with anti-dam movement.
    • They had to reframe their movement to talk about land struggle and ecology.
