Keck, Margaret and Kathryn Sikkink. 1998. Activists beyond borders: Advocacy networks in international politics

Notes from reviews

  1. Polletta in CS
    • "Keck and Sikkink argue that transnational advocacy networks have proliferated in recent decades as a result of sophisticated communication technologies, the activist legacy of the 1960s, and the global spread of a normative discourse of human rights."
    • Language matters, insofar as it resonants with larger publics
    • The networks promoting causes and the vulnerability of the target group to moral and material pressure also matter.
    • They do not connect domestic movements and advocacy networks.
    • There is no theory of the mass media in this volume.
  2. Smith in APSR
    • Networks are composed of nonstate actors, NGOs, foundations, sympathetic individuals serving in government and intergovernment agencies.
    • Individuals' participation in international political processes has important political implications. It affects their views of global problems and politics and advances their skills for participation in these processes.
