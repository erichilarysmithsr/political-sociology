Macy, Michael W. 1990. "Learning Theory and the Logic of Critical Mass." ASR 55(6).

  1. Introduction
    • Oliver-Marwell breaking Olson's selective incentives model.
  2. A stochastic learning model of critical mass
    • Extending the theory of critical mass by relaxing four strong constraints in the original formulation: rational actors; decisions are isolated events; outcomes are deterministic; and pure jointness of supply
    • Learning and rationality
      • Generally cannot expect full rationality.
      • Outcomes of social interaction operate as positive and negative sanctions or social cues that guide actors toward anomie or solidarity through a process of stochastic search, based on reinforcement and attenuation of cooperative propensities.
      • This view extends theory of critical mass in three ways -
        1. reduces cognitive demands on the actors - instead of being "forward-looking" actors, they can be "backward-looking" - they tend to repeat choices that seem to pay and avoid those that prove costly.
        2. applies to behavior based on normative as well as instrumental concerns - moves can be rule-governed, and not just pragmatic. "Enthusiasm for participation reflects the experience of collective rather than personal efficacy." (812)
        3. applies to situations where rational actors may be trapped by strategic "gaming"
    • Serial decisions, uncertainty, and social leverage
      • Need to relax three further assumptions - events are discrete, processes are deterministic, and public goods are pure and collectively profitable.
    • Need to further specify the production function and the learning algorithm
