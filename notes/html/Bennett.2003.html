Bennett, W. Lance. 2003. "Communicating Global Activism: Some Strengths and Vulnerabilities of Networked Politics." In Cyberprotest: New Media, Citizens and Social Movements.

  1. Introduction
    • Exploring activist communication practices both in terms of political effects and implications for SM organization and mobilization.
  2. Communication as a key to understanding global activism
    • Organization and communication patterns in global activist networks, use of the Internet as a public sphere for exchanging ideas independent of top-down mediation.
    • Internet may facilitate network-building based on affinities or relatively loose identifications.
    • But they may reduce ideological definition and decision-making coherence.
  3. Social change and global politics
    • Growing freedom to form identifications outside of dominant institutions, what Bennett has called lifestyle politics.
  4. Communication in activist networks: political strategy and organizational resource
    • Permanent campaigns - global activism is characterized by long-running communication campaigns to organize protest and publicize issues aimed at transnational orgs, corporations, and other targets
    • Communication in diverse networks is ideologically thin
    • Internet use can affect the organization of global activist networks
    • New media can alter information flows through mass media
